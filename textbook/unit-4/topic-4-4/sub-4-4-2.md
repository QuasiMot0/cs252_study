## Why Coalesce?

When a block is freed, its physical neighbors in memory may also be free. Leaving them as separate small blocks causes **external fragmentation** — the total free space could satisfy a large request, but no single block is large enough. Coalescing merges adjacent free blocks into one larger block.

Boundary tags make this O(1): right neighbor = `(char*)block + block->size`, left neighbor = `(char*)block - block->left_size`.

## The 4 Coalescing Cases

When freeing block X:

### Case 1: Neither neighbor is free

Both the left block and the right block are allocated (or are fenceposts).

**Action**: Simply insert X into the appropriate free list (at the front).

### Case 2: Only the right neighbor is free

The right block R is free; the left block is allocated.

**Action**:
1. Remove R from its free list using R's `next`/`prev` pointers.
2. Merge X and R: `X->size += R->size`.
3. Update the block after R: its `left_size = X->size` (the merged size).
4. Insert the merged block where R was in the free list (or move to a new list if size changed).

### Case 3: Only the left neighbor is free

The left block L is free; the right block is allocated.

**Action**:
1. Remove L from its free list.
2. Merge L and X: `L->size += X->size`.
3. Update the block after X: its `left_size = L->size` (the merged size).
4. Insert the merged block where L was in the free list (or move to appropriate list if size changed).

### Case 4: Both neighbors are free

Both the left block L and the right block R are free.

**Action**:
1. Remove L from its free list.
2. Remove R from its free list.
3. Merge L, X, and R: `L->size += X->size + R->size`.
4. Update the block after R: its `left_size = L->size`.
5. Insert the merged block where L was in the free list (or move if size changed).

From the lab spec: "The coalesced block should remain where the left block (lower in memory) was in the free list."

## Updating Free-List Membership

After coalescing, the merged block may have grown beyond its original size class. The lab spec says:

> "When coalescing a block, if the size of the coalesced block is no longer appropriate for the current list, the newly formed block should be removed and inserted into the appropriate free list."

The exception: for the last list (`N_LISTS-1`), you never need to remove — all large blocks belong there regardless of size.

## Free Algorithm Steps (from slides)

1. **Check left neighbor** (via `left_size`): if free, remove from free list and coalesce.
2. **Check right neighbor** (via `size`): if free, remove from free list and coalesce.
3. **Insert** the (possibly merged) freed block into the correct free list, and **update headers** of the freed block and the block immediately to its right.

## HW1 Examples

**Q5** (neither neighbor free): `free(block4)` — block4 goes directly into free list. size=32.

**Q6** (left neighbor free): `free(block4)` when block3 is free → coalesce block3+block4 → merged block3 at offset `...0064`, size=64. Stays where block3 was in list.

**Q7** (left neighbor free): `free(block2)` when block1 is free → coalesce block1+block2 → merged block1 at offset `...0000`, size=64. Stays where block1 was.

**Q8** (both neighbors free): `free(block2)` when block1 and block3 are free → coalesce block1+block2+block3 → merged at `...0000`, size=96. Stays where block1 (lower in memory) was.

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Coalescing | Merging a freed block with adjacent free neighbors |
| 4 cases | Neither free; right only; left only; both free |
| Remove from free list | Using `next`/`prev` to splice a block out of its doubly-linked list in O(1) |
| left_size update | After merging, the block after the merged block must have its `left_size` updated |
